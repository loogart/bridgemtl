The process

  • A. Signature of agreement and payment of USD 10,000
  • B. Adapt your business plan.

    The first step we work on together is to take your business plan and adapt it to the local market and immigration authorities’ prerequisites and guidelines. This adapted business plan will be used in your immigration file request, as well as in your application to the BridgeMTL Incubator.

  • C. Validate your project.

    The BridgeMTL Incubator has set up a selection committee made up of experts and seasoned entrepreneurs to validate your project and ensure that your business plan meets the high standards of the Ministry of Immigration (MIFI). This committee will work with you and make recommendations that must be observed and followed before obtaining the approval of the latter and proceeding to the next step, the immigration application supervised by our legal department

  • D. Payment of USD 5,000
  • E. Documentation gathering and application submission to Quebec Immigration Authorities (“MIFI”).

    At this point, you have a viable business plan, it now time to focus on the rest of your immigration application. BridgeMTL’s immigration legal team will complete it and will need your collaboration to fill gaps in the information requested from the immigration authorities. In parallel, BridgeMTL Incubator will fill an official Service Offer, required in the application.

  • F. Review by authorities

    The immigration authorities will review your file and allocate a file number.

  • H. Selection interview and/or prospective trip in Quebec.

    You may be convened to assist a selection interview in Montreal assisted by the lawyer at BridgeMTL. You can also visit Montreal/Quebec to explore the local market, all supervised by BridgeMTL.

  • Decision

    Upon review, the immigration agent will either accept your file as is or ask for clarifications. In such case, the BridgeMTL legal will assist in answering questions to assure the file is accepted. The immigration agent may also refuse the file. In that case, if the BridgeMTL legal department believes that the MIFI has failed to meet its obligations, an administrative appeal will be submitted to overturn the outcome at no additional fees.

  • I. C.S.Q. issuance and permanent residence application

    The Quebec government, upon acceptance, will issue a CSQ (Certificat de selection du Québec), which means they accept your file, and your file is forwarded to the Canadian government (IRCC) for permanent residence and work permit if required.

  • J. Payment of USD 5,000
  • K. Visa for visit.

    If a visa is necessary for a visit to Montreal, the BridgeMTL legal team will apply for the visitor visa for you, at no extra cost. If you wish to come to Montreal to work, they can also request a work visa, also at no extra fee.

  • L. Business project implementation.

    When you arrive in Quebec, all services of the Incubator start being delivered. From a personal perspective, we will help you as needed to find a temporary or permanent home, schools for your children, suggestions and recommendations of all sorts. From a business perspective, you now have access to your office in the historic center of the city of Montreal, as well as help with financing, coaching, networking, business development, etc.

  • M. Payment of USD 10,000, upon arrival
  • N. One year of access to various business professionals.

    For the next 12 months, you will go through the Incubation program. Our team will allocate all its resources to make sure your business plan is converted into reality. In parallel, BridgeMTL’s legal team will request your Permanent Residency with the Canadian immigration authorities.

  • O. Final payment of USD 10,000, 6 months after arrival.
  • P. BridgeMTL Alumni.

    P. BridgeMTL Alumni.

Total cost: USD 40,000* not including government fees, official documents to translate and notarize, etc.
